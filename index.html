ME2 Computing Coursework Project, Feb 2020

2D Heat Diffusion Model

Worked as a pair to analyse the heat transfer through a hot long rectangular rod when quenched in a cold fluid. To analyse, we aimed to model the temperature distribution through the rod as it changes in time, with Python. The rod is a composition of two different materials; in this case we chose brick and steel (AISI 1010) although it can be easily changed to fit any composition. The rod is long enough that the heat transfer along the longest length can be neglected, so the heat transfer was modelled in two space dimensions and time. The square cross-sectional length of the rod is L (1m for the model shown here). The location and area of the secondary material was chosen to be a small rectangle, slightly offset from the centre. However, this can easily be adjusted accordingly to model different problems. The rod is cooled for a total time of t seconds.

We assumed uniform density, uniform specific heat and no internal heat sources. This model can be described by a 3rd order parabolic partial derivative equation.

Summer Project, Ongoing

Object Detection App

Work in progress! How cool would it be for everyone to have a car distance alerter and sign-board reader while driving without the need of any inbuilt sesors. Thats when I had the idea of making an app anyone could download which would utilise the phone's camera to perform the required tasks. The basic idea relies on machine learning to detect the exact model of the car infront. Then, based on the screen real-estate taken up by the car, the app could work out the distance.

The only issue was that I knew nothing object detection and neural networks. So I decided that learning about these would be a good place to start. So far, using OpenCV on Python, I've made a few basic programs all of which work in real-time using a camera. These include a basic motion detector, road lane-detector and a generic frontal face detector.

The next step would be learning to make a mobile application and I'll try to keep this page updated with any new progress.

Code2Care Hackathon hosted by Imperial Business School, Jul 2020

CheckApp

As a group of four, second year mechanical engineering students, we participated in the annual 5-day hackathon hosted by Imperial College Business School. This year's competition involved finding a sustainable healthtech solution to tackle the global non-communicable disease epidemic.

Our idea was to increase NCD awareness amongst the general population by implementing an app called CheckApp. The app would take user inputted health data and from other mobile health apps. There would also be an optional prompt to input any family health data.

By generating a family tree, the app could calculate potential future genetic based risks. As most NCDs are not genetically but environmentally dependent, the app would also calculate the external factor risk using machine learning from other user data.
